Influences on Our Constitution

 

Ancient Judaism      

·       The laws of Moses (Torah of Moses) refers to the “five books” that were used to set the precedent for law including: moral laws, social laws, and food laws.

•       The influence of Biblical laws can be found in the legal and ethical codes throughout different aspects of national, state, & local governments.

•       Moses stressed that people should seek to create a just society based on respect for the law.

•       Colonial thinkers based their notion of justice on this idea

•       Colonists, who were influenced by their faith, believed in natural law, the idea that a universal set of moral principles existed.

•       Many colonists believed that a human law that violated natural law was unjust and should be changed.

Ancient:

u  Greeks – Ancient Greeks introduced the idea of direct democracy, or decision making by all citizens.

u  Direct democracy took root in New England’s town meetings, where citizens gathered to solve local problems.

Romans – From ancient Romans came the idea of representative government, or decision-making by elected officials. This idea would be the basis of US government.

u  Government Structure Diagram: The Roman Republic's government was divided into 3 different sections - each section had different powers.
