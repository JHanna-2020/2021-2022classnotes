Fundamentals of Economics

·      Economics is the study of how people and nations use limited resources to try to satisfy wants and needs.

 

·      Governments play key roles in economies, and every economy has producers, distributors, consumers, labor, resources, and capital.

                                                                                                                         

·      Economic systems are classified either by how the economies work or by the political ideology that is connected to the economy.

 

The Role of Government in Economic Systems

·      Mixed economy combine elements of capitalism and socialism.

 

·      Socialism is an economic system in which government owns the basic means of production, determines the use of resources, distributes products and wages, and provides social services for its people (health care and welfare).

 

·      Democratic socialists believe in political democracy but want government to regulate the distribution of wealth.

 

·      Communism is an economic system in which the central government directs all major economic decisions.

 

·      Government decides how much to produce, what to produce, and how to distribute goods and services.

 

·      A communist system is called a command economy because the government makes the decisions for the nation.

 

·      In a purely communist country, there is no private property — the state owns the land, natural resources, industry, banks, and transportation facilities and also controls mass communication.

 

·      Free Enterprise System / Capitalism – An economic system characterized by private or corporate ownership  of capital goods; investments that are determined by private decision rather than by state control; and determined in a free market.

 

·      The four fundamental factors of the free enterprise system? Private ownership, individual initiative, profit, and competition

 

•       Free enterprise does not rely on government to decide what items are produced, or how much any item is to sell for. Those decisions are made by the market through the law of supply and demand. Like democracy, it is based on the concept of individual freedom.

 

·      Government’s involvement in the free enterprise system is to protect the public and preserve private enterprise. The great debate is how much should government participate.
