Albany Plan of the Union

1.     The Albany Plan of Union was a proposal made at the Albany Congress back in 1754 aimed at a formation of a strong union of the colonies under one single government and direction.

 

2.     The need was justified because of the necessity for defense against the threats and consequences posed by the infamous French and Indian War.

 

3.     It was proposed by Benjamin Franklin and was among the many plans presented by the different delegates that participated in the Albany Congress.

 

4.     The proposal called for a general government that will be administered by a President General appointed and fully supported by the Crown.

 

 

5.     Once the Albany Congress approved the plan, copies were sent to colonial assemblies and to the British Board of Trade in London

 

 

6.     Among the issues that could well derail national development would be Finance, control of commerce activities, national defense, and most especially, dealing with the different Indian Tribes.

7.     Why were the two sides against the plan?         

·       Most of the people, and the colonial assemblies in general are thinking backwards and very suspicious of the idea of a Central Taxing Authority.

·       On the other side of the line, many personalities in the British government are never in favor of a consolidation of additional power at their disposal.

 

8.     The British government instead preferred that colonists should focus more on  the planned military campaign.   

 

9.     The Board of Trade for their part, never sought any official approval from the Crown. They instead proposed that the colonial governors and their respective councils should facilitate in the efforts of raising armies and the building of forts.

 

10.  The money needed for these activities will come from the Treasury of Great Britain, and would be later reimbursed through an Act of Parliament that would tax America.

 

***The most important thing to come out of the Albany meeting was a plan for another meeting..

Stamp Act Congress

11.  Why was the Stamp Act Congress created? No Taxation without Representation

12.  James Otis suggested an intercolonial conference to agree on a united course of action

 

13.  The Stamp Act Congress convened in New York in October of 1765.

14.  This meeting seem to be a failure at first because

(1) Only nine of the colonies sent delegates

(2) The congress became quickly divided between the radicals and moderates

15.  What was the focus of the Stamp Act Resolves? Taxation

16.  Who is William Ruggles? The president of the Congress

17.  In the end, the widespread boycotts enacted by individual colonists surely did more to secure the repeal of the Stamp Act than did the Congress itself.

 

18.  Why was this gathering significant? For the first time, respected delegates from differing colonies sat with each other and engaged in spirited debate.

19.  What did they discover in this meeting?

in many ways they had more in common than they originally had thought
